# Assumptions and Initial Premises

The cost of a new apartment in **Mexico City (Narvarte Poniente neighborhood)** is **MXN 3,750,000**.

The mortgage simulation was performed on **March 13, 2026** using the **BBVA platform**, obtaining the following conditions:

- **Loan amount:** 70% – MXN 2,625,000  
- **Minimum down payment:** MXN 1,125,000  
- **Annual interest rate:** 10.90%  
- **Monthly payment:** MXN 30,168  
- **Required monthly income:** MXN 82,821  
- **APR (CAT):** 13.3% (excluding VAT)

These figures **do not include additional costs**, such as:

- **Notary and legal fees:** approximately **8% – 15%** of the total property value.
- **Title registration (escrituración):** between **3% and 6%** of the property value.
- **Utility installations and service contracts**, such as:
  - Electricity (CFE)
  - Gas
  - Water
  - Internet

All expenses incurred during the transaction will be incorporated into the final model, including **taxes related to the property acquisition**.

# Rental Assumptions

To estimate rental income, we use the general rule that **monthly rent typically ranges between 0.8% and 1.2% of the total property value**.

For this case:

- **Estimated monthly rent:** MXN 18,000  
- **Maintenance fee:** MXN 1,000  
- **Total monthly income:** **MXN 19,000**

# Model Considerations

The model must incorporate the following assumptions:

- The property is located in **Narvarte Poniente**, a consolidated residential area.
- Annual operational expenses must be prorated, including:
  - Property tax (**Predial**)
  - Owner-related repairs and maintenance
- **Average occupancy rate:** 90% (the property will not always be rented).
- **Annual rent increases** will follow the **Consumer Price Index (CPI)**.
- **Property price appreciation** in Narvarte Poniente will be validated to adjust expected **capital gains**.

# FIBRA Portfolio Comparison

To make the comparison realistic, a **FIBRA portfolio** will be constructed using:

- **Initial capital:** MXN 825,000 (equivalent to the property down payment)

Monthly contributions will replicate the **cash flow required to maintain the property investment**.

Example:

Mortgage payment:

30,168 + VAT

Rental income:

18,000 + 1,000 maintenance

The **additional monthly contribution to the FIBRA portfolio** would therefore be:

11,168 MXN

This approach attempts to maintain a **fair and objective comparison between real estate ownership and REIT investments (FIBRAs)**.

However, it should be validated whether the correct methodology is:

- investing **only the net difference**, or  
- investing the **full mortgage payment amount**.

# FIBRA Portfolio Investment Criteria

The FIBRA portfolio must follow these guidelines:

- **Occupancy rate above 90%**, except for the hotel sector.
- Do **not purchase overvalued FIBRAs**, meaning:
  - Market price must not exceed **NAV / CBFI (Net Asset Value)**.
- Avoid FIBRAs with **dividend yields below 6%–7%**, since this would be lower than **Mexican government bonds (CETES)**.
- The investment horizon will be **3 years**.
- If a FIBRA becomes **significantly overvalued**, part of the position should be trimmed depending on the degree of overvaluation.
- **Debt ratio must be below 40%**, ideally lower.
- **Dividend sustainability is mandatory**:
  - **AFFO must be greater than the payout ratio**.
  - If not, the FIBRA may be using reserves to pay dividends, which is undesirable even if the yield appears attractive.
